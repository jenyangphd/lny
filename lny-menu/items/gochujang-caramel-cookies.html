Gochujang Caramel Cookies

The Columbian Exchange gave Korea chili peppers. Korea gave back gochujang.

Jang is the term for Korea’s foundational fermented soybean-based condiments: ganjang (soy sauce), doenjang (soybean paste), and gochujang (fermented chili paste). These three jangs form the backbone of Korean cuisine, and the jang tradition dates back to 57 BC–668 CE, representing one of the world’s oldest fermentation cultures. Gochujang is the newest of the three, not having developed until after chili peppers arrived on the Korean peninsula via Japan in the late 16th century by way of Portuguese trade routes.

Fun Fact

Jangdokdae (the elevated stone platform on which jang was made in earthenware jars) was seen as the spiritual center of the Korean home and positioned to catch optimal sunlight.

Not-So-Fun Fact

After the Korean War, industrialization and American-influenced development policy accelerated urbanization, disrupting multi-generational jang-making traditions. Home fermentation was largely replaced by factory production by the 1970s.
