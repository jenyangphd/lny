Du-Jjon-Ku-Covered Strawberries

An artifact of the K-pop industrial complex.

Du-jjon-ku is a portmanteau of “Dubai,” “chewy” (jjondeuk), and “cookie”—a Korean creation inspired by 2024’s Dubai chocolate trend, combining marshmallow, kataifi pastry, and pistachio cream.

Korean café culture sits at an interesting intersection of postwar American influence, Japanese imperialism, global capital, and social media. Contemporary Korean bakery culture has evolved into something entirely its own: a hyper-competitive, trend-driven ecosystem where bakeries function as destinations, social media content generators, and cultural signifiers. The K-pop industrial complex plays a direct role—celebrity posts can trigger nationwide sellouts and ingredient shortages within days. This is food culture shaped not by tradition or terroir, but by algorithmic attention, parasocial relationships with pop stars, and the economics of delivery apps.
